Siddha

Siddha is one of the oldest traditional systems of medicine, believed to have been developed in Tamil Nadu, India. It is based on the teachings of Siddhars, ancient saints, who practiced and perfected the system over centuries. Siddha medicine is primarily focused on the balance between the three humors—vatha, pitta, and kapha—and uses natural elements like herbs, minerals, and metals to restore health and prevent disease. The system emphasizes the importance of spiritual well-being in achieving physical health and believes in treating both the body and the mind. Siddha is popular in South India and is recognized for its unique treatments and healing practices.
